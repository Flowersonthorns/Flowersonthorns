Our Programs

Flowers On Thorns CBO runs structured programs targeting vulnerable youth and young mothers in Roysambu Sub-county and its surroundings. Our interventions are guided by the needs of the community and inspired by lived experiences of recovery, resilience, and hope.

1. Psychosocial Support

We offer safe spaces for healing through one-on-one counseling, group therapy, trauma healing, and mental wellness support. Most of our beneficiaries have survived gender-based violence, addiction, or abandonment. The psychosocial program is foundational to all our other services.

2. Vocational and Skills Training

We equip youth with employable and business skills to enable economic independence. Trainings are offered in partnership with technical trainers and institutions. Current skill areas include:

  • Hairdressing and Beauty Therapy
  • Tailoring and Fashion Design
  • Catering and Baking
  • Parenting and Life Skills

3. Economic Empowerment

We support youth and mothers to start income-generating activities after training. Beneficiaries receive business mentorship, startup kits, or referrals to microfinance partners. Some have started salons, grocery shops, food stalls, or tailoring businesses near their homes.

4. Life Skills & Mentorship

We train youth on self-awareness, self-esteem, decision-making, leadership, communication, and reproductive health. Peer mentorship and community role models guide them on behavior change and long-term planning.

5. Care Packages & Community Outreach

We provide care packages and support to vulnerable families including foodstuffs, clothing, sanitary towels, baby items, and school uniforms. Outreach is conducted in slums, informal settlements, and during community forums.

Our Impact

  • Over 300 youth reached through awareness and outreach since 2015
  • More than 120 trained in vocational skills and psychosocial healing
  • Dozens of small businesses launched by graduates from the program
  • 10+ strategic partners including training institutions, churches, and CBOs

We continue to measure our impact by tracking growth in mental wellness, employability, community reintegration, and household stability.
